Espresso is one of the most widely drunk and sought after coffee types. it consists of having a compressed ‘puck’ of coffee grinds inside of a handle often called a portafilter. These grounds then have near boiling high pressure water pushed through them. This results in a concentrated coffee drink with a layer of bubbly foam on top referred to as crema. This is considered one of the purest ways to taste coffee so is what lots of people aim for in a coffee set up. Espresso, as it is so strong and produces so little liquid, is then often diluted. If diluted with hot water this is called a long black or an americano. However you can dilute your espresso with milk in a variety of ways to create many drinks like lattes, cappucinos, flat whites, etc.
  1. Grind a weight of coffee based on your machines manual
  2. Put the ground coffee into your portafilter
  3. (optional) Distribute the coffee using a distribution tool
    1. if this is a wedge tool, simply spin it on top of the ground coffee
    2. If it is a needle tool, stir the coffee around until there are no more clumps
  4. Use your tamp to compress the coffee down. You can't press too hard in this situation so just try to press out as much air as you can
  5. To put your portafilter into the machine. Start at a slight angle until you feel the handle go further into the machine then twist until the handle is perpendicular to your body
  6. Immediately press your shot button
  7. If you have a timed machine then simply wait for the shot to run and then follow on.
    1. Otherwise, press the button again once you have reached the desired weight of coffee. For two shots this is typically 36g, then half that for a single shot
  8. After your coffee is extracted, move whatever cup your coffee has been brewed into and then remove the portafilter by doing the opposite action to getting it in
  9. To empty the portafilter you can do one of two ways, if you have a dump box, one firm knock onto the bar should help the coffee leave the portafilter, if you have no dump box you can just use a spoon to scoop the grounds out. You can dispose of your groundfs into the bin or compost as coffee grounds make a great fertiliser
  10. Rinse the portafilter out, and put back into your machine like you are making another shot
  11. You have now successfully brewed some delicious espresso, you can now:
    1. Pour over a cup of hot water to create a long black
    2. pour in some textured milk on top to create a latte
    3. with less froth you can make a flat white
  12. There are endless combinations to try!
  13. Congratulations! You have just become your very own barista
